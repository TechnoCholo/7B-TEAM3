Introduction

The skeletal system is your body's central support framework. In adults, it consists of 206 bones and performs many important functions including support, movement, protection, blood cell production and mineral storage.

Skeletal System
The skeletal system is your body's central support structure. It consists of bones, cartilage, ligaments and tendons.

Its five major functions are:
  • Support
  • Protection
  • Movement
  • Blood Cell Production
  • Mineral Storage

Bone Diseases

Osteoporosis

A disease that weakens bones by decreasing bone mass and density. Bones become fragile and more likely to break.


Osteogenesis Imperfecta

A genetic condition that causes bones to break easily.


Paget's Disease

Interferes with the normal recycling process of bone tissue.

Types of Bones

Long Bones

Support weight and help you move. Examples: arms and legs.


Short Bones

Provide stability and small movements. Examples: wrists and ankles.


Flat Bones

Protect inner organs like a shield. Examples: skull and ribs.

Irregular Bones

Complex shapes with custom jobs. Examples: spine and pelvis.


Sesamoid Bones

Small bones embedded in tendons. Example: kneecaps.

Parts of the Bone

Compact Bone: Hard outer layer. Supports body weight.


Spongy Bone: Honeycomb inner layer. Lightens the skeleton.


Red Marrow: Produces all blood cells.


Yellow Marrow: Fatty central tissue. Stores energy reserves.


Arteries and Veins: Deliver nutrients and remove waste.

Fun Facts

Born With More Bones

Babies are born with around 300 bones, but adults have only 206 because many bones fuse together during growth.

Constant Renewal

The skeleton completely replaces its own cells approximately every 10 years.

Crowded Extremities

Over half of the bones in the human body are located in the hands and feet.
